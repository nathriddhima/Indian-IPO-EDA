## 📌 Objective
To perform an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on 561 Indian IPOs listed between 2010 and 2025, identifying key patterns in listing day performance, subscription trends, and issue size impact, providing actionable insights for retail investors.

---

## 🗂️ Dataset
- **Source:** Kaggle — IPO Data India 2010–2025
- **Records:** 561 IPOs
- **Columns:** Date, IPO Name, Issue Size (Crores), QIB, HNI, RII, Total Subscription, Offer Price, List Price, Listing Gain (%), CMP (BSE/NSE), Current Gains (%)

## 📊 Key Visualizations & Findings

### 1️⃣ Top 10 IPOs by Listing Day Gain
- **Sigachi Industries Limited** delivered the highest listing day gain of 250%+
- Defence and pharmaceutical sectors dominated the top 10
- Small to mid-cap companies consistently outperformed large-cap IPOs on listing day

### 2️⃣ Average IPO Listing Gain by Year (2010–2025)
- **2014** was the strongest year, driven by post-election bull sentiment
- **2016–2018** saw a significant dip, impact of Demonetization on investor confidence
- **2021–2022** witnessed a strong surge Post-COVID bull run (Zomato, Paytm era)
- **2025** shows a cooling trend as markets stabilize

### 3️⃣ IPO Issue Size vs Listing Gain
- **Small IPOs (below ₹500 Cr)** show high variance, potential for 150–250% gains
- **Large IPOs (above ₹2000 Cr)** consistently list closer to offer price (0–50% range)
- **Key Insight:** Smaller IPOs carry higher risk but offer significantly better listing day returns

### 4️⃣ Total Subscription vs Listing Gain
- Strong positive correlation between subscription rate and listing gains
- IPOs subscribed 150x+ consistently delivered higher listing day performance
- **Key Insight:** Subscription rate especially QIB portion is a reliable predictor of listing day success

### 5️⃣ Distribution of IPO Listing Gains
- Distribution is **right-skewed**, majority of IPOs list at 0–30% gains
- Exceptional gainers (150–250%) are more frequent than equivalent losers
- **Key Insight:** IPO investing has a favorable risk-reward profile for retail investors

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
1. **Focus on small-cap IPOs** — they offer the highest potential listing day gains
2. **Track QIB subscription** — heavy institutional interest is the strongest signal for listing performance
3. **Avoid mega IPOs for listing day gains** — large issue sizes tend to underperform on listing day
4. **Timing matters** — bull market years (2014, 2021) deliver significantly better average IPO returns
